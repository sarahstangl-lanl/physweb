RSS

Really Simple Syndication (RSS) is an XML-based format for content distribution. The School of Physics and Astronomy offers several RSS feeds for use in news readers and Web logs (blogs). These feeds include headlines, summaries and links back to www.physics.umn.edu for the full article.

How to sign up for School of Physics and Astronomy RSS feeds

Click on the content area(s) below you're interested in subscribing to, and follow the instructions to add to your news reader.

Calendar - Weekly calendar feed
News - Departmental News
Outreach - Outreach Highlights
Publications - Recent Publications

Feeds are also available for current Physics classes, from the class index page.

How to access RSS feeds

There are a number of ways to access RSS feeds. You can install a news reader that displays RSS feeds from the Web sites you select, enabling you to view hundreds of headlines at once. After installing the news reader, you can add each feed manually from the Web site by clicking on the "Subscribe" or the "XML" orange button next to the feed.

An alternative to downloading a dedicated news reader is to use a Web-based news reader. For example, My Yahoo! or My AOL users can now add RSS feeds directly to their personal page.
